The Gateway of India

The **Gateway of India** is one of Mumbai's most famous monuments. Located on the waterfront at Apollo Bunder, it was built to commemorate the landing of King George V and Queen Mary in 1911. The monument is a popular gathering place for locals and tourists alike, offering a beautiful view of the Arabian Sea. It is a striking example of Indo-Saracenic architecture, with its design combining elements of Hindu and Muslim styles.

The monument was also the place from which the last British troops departed from India in 1948, marking the end of the British rule. Today, the area around the Gateway is a vibrant hub, with vendors, photographers, and visitors enjoying the sea breeze and the view of the iconic **Taj Mahal Palace Hotel** nearby.
