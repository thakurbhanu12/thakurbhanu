Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CSMT)

Formerly known as Victoria Terminus, **CSMT** is a **UNESCO World Heritage Site** and a historic railway station. Its stunning Victorian Gothic Revival architecture makes it one of the most beautiful buildings in the city. The station was designed by British architect Frederick William Stevens and was completed in 1888. Its grand design features intricate stone carvings, domes, turrets, and a large central dome, which is a major highlight.

CSMT is a bustling transport hub that serves as a gateway for millions of commuters daily. It is a symbol of Mumbai's connectivity and colonial past, and its grandeur has made it a popular backdrop for many Bollywood films. Despite its historical significance, it remains one of the busiest and most efficient railway stations in the world.
